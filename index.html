New York Overcomes

The interesting results can be found in the state of New York. New York is the fourth largest state with 19.7 million people and home to the city with the largest population, which should be disastrous during a pandemic. However, New York has only had 789,000 cases compared to the 857,000 cases in Illinois, with a population of 12.9 million.

A line graph of the confirmed COVID-19 cases in California, Texas, and New York. Includes a toggle to display as weekly or daily cases and a toggle to display COVID-19 Deaths.

Looking at the start of the pandemic, New York experienced a disproportionate number of infections and deaths. The number of infections can primarily be attributed to the densely populated New York city, which overloaded their healthcare infrastructure resulting in many deaths. What is unexpected is how New York has handled the pandemic following the first wave of cases.

New York drastically reduced the number of new cases and deaths. The state was able to completely avoid the second wave of infections, where other states seen an increase of infections compared to the initial wave. While New York is currently experiencing the third wave, it has been able to retain infections similar to the first wave with a fraction of the deaths. Meanwhile, California and Texas are developing more infections with each new wave.
