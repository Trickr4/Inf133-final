CDC Recommendations

Wash Your Hands Often

  • After you have been in a public place
  • After blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing
  • Before eating or preparing food
  • Beofre touching your face
  • After using the restroom
  • After handling your mask
  • After caring for someone sick

If soap and water are not readily available, use a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol. It is important to avoid touching your face with unwashed hands.

Social Distance

Be sure to maintain a 6 foot distance between yourself and others. If you are inside your own home, be sure to avoid close contact with any sick members in the household. If you are outside your home, be sure to avoid close contact with those outside of your household.

Wear a Mask

Be sure to wear a mask to help prevent the spread of the virus. To properly wear your mask, your mouth and nose should be covered. Wearing your mask, however, is not a substitute to social distancing. Please continue keeping a 6 foot distance from others when needed. Cover Coughs and Sneezes

Always cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or the inside of your elbow when coughing or sneezing. If you are using a tissue, try to throw the tissue away in a garbage can immediately after. Try to wash your hands after. If there is no soap and water readily available, use hand sanatizer.

Clean and Disinfect

Try to clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces daily including:

  • tables
  • doorknobs
  • countertops
  • phones
  • sinks
  • etc.

If surfaces are dirty, be sure to clean the area with detergent or soap and water before disinfecting with a household disinfectant.

Monitor your Health Daily

Make sure to stay alert for symptoms of Covid-19. If you are showing symptoms, take your temperature. When taking your temperature, be sure not to take it within 30 minutes of exercising or taking medication that could lower your temperature.

Symptoms of Covid-19

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headaches
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus

Anyone can have mild to severe symptoms

Older adults and people with severe underlying medical conditions may be at risk for developing serious complications.
